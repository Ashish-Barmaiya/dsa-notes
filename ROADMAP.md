# DSA Roadmap

> **Goal:** Build a deep understanding of Data Structures & Algorithms from first principles, develop strong problem-solving intuition.

---

# Philosophy

For every topic, the objective is to understand:

* What it is
* Why it exists
* How it works internally
* When it should be used
* Its trade-offs
* Its time and space complexity
* How to implement it from scratch
* How it appears in interview problems
* How it is used in real-world systems

---

# Learning Principles

Every topic follows the same learning process.

```
Theory
    ↓
Internal Working
    ↓
Complexity Analysis
    ↓
Implementation from Scratch
    ↓
Standard Library Support
    ↓
Problem Solving
    ↓
Pattern Recognition
    ↓
Real World Applications
    ↓
Review & Revision
```

# For Every Topic

Every topic should answer the following questions:

* What problem does it solve?
* Why was it invented?
* How does it work internally?
* What are its time complexities?
* What are its space complexities?
* What are the common operations?
* What are the trade-offs?
* How do I implement it from scratch?
* How is it supported in Go?
* What interview patterns rely on it?
* Where is it used in production systems?

---

# Completion Criteria

A topic is considered complete only when all of the following are done:

* Theory understood
* Internal working documented
* Complexity analyzed
* Implemented from scratch in Go
* Notes written
* Common patterns identified
* Representative problems solved
* Mistakes documented
* Real-world applications documented
* Reviewed after a delay

---

# Guiding Principle

> **The objective is not to solve the maximum number of problems. The objective is to develop the ability to derive efficient solutions for problems that have never been seen before.**
